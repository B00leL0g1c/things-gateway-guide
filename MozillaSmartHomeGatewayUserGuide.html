I. Introduction

Your Smart Home Gateway by Mozilla is an alpha prototype, often referred to as the “Things Gateway”. The gateway lets you directly monitor and control your home over the web, often bypassing the need to purchase separate hubs and download apps for each  different manufacturer.

This guide will explain how to set up your gateway, connect smart home devices, create rules to automate your home, plus a few additional tips.

A. Things Gateway Running on Raspberry Pi

The Things Gateway software can be installed onto an 8GB or larger microSD card which is inserted into a Raspberry Pi. We recommend the latest model, such as the RPi 3 B+. You can optionally attach Zigbee and Z-wave radios that connect to the RPi over USB.

B. Flashing the microSD Card

First download the Things Gateway image from here. We recommend using the etcher.io program to flash the image onto the microSD card that is inserted into your computer. Alternatively, some distributors sell the Raspberry Pi with a flashed microSD card containing the gateway software image pre-flashed and ready for setup.

II. First Time Setup

In order for your Mozilla Things Gateway to communicate with your home network, a one-time connection setup process must be completed between the Things Gateway and your home network.

A. Power Up

1. Make sure the microSD card is plugged into the Raspberry Pi, and optionally plug USB dongles, such as Zigbee and Z-wave, into one of the four USB ports. The additional USB dongles  allows the gateway to communicate with your smart home devices that don’t use Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or other IP networking protocols. 

2. Plug the gateway into a power outlet using the provided USB power supply. Wait 2-3 minutes for it to power up before proceeding to the next step.

B. Connect to Wi-Fi

3. Using your laptop, connect to the Wi-Fi network called “Mozilla IoT Gateway”.

TIP: If you don’t see “Mozilla IoT Gateway” in your device’s list of Wi-Fi networks, check your Things Gateway. Make sure the microSD card is securely pushed into the underside of your gateway and try reconnecting the power.

From the welcome screen, select your home Wi-Fi and enter your Wi-Fi password to connect the gateway to your network.

TIP: If you don’t see the welcome screen, make sure you are connected to “Mozilla IoT Gateway”, then try typing 192.168.220.1 into your web browser’s address bar to navigate to the page.

C. Choose a Web Address

Your Mozilla Things Gateway is now connected to your home network. Next, give your gateway its own name and create an account so that you can access it and control your smart home devices remotely and securely.

4. Type http://gateway.local into your web browser’s address bar to find your gateway from your home network. If it does not load, you can type the IP address instead. Find the IP address by typing “ping gateway.local” on your computer (use “ping -4 gateway.local” if using Windows 10).

(example IP address)

TIP: If http://gateway.local or http://<IP_address> can’t be found, check to make sure your laptop is connected to your home wi-fi network (not the “Mozilla IoT Gateway” network). If you can log into your home Wi-Fi router, you can look up the gateway IP address by seeing the name “gateway” or  finding the MAC address starting with “b8:...”

5. Choose a unique web address that can be used to securely access your Things Gateway over the Internet. Choose a name, enter your preferred email, and select “Create.”

6. Finally, create an account to access your gateway. This will allow you to securely access your gateway and its devices from any web browser. (Other accounts can be added for additional household members later in Settings > Users, and follow a similar sign-up process.)

TIP: Use a strong password when creating an account for your Things Gateway to minimize unwanted access and control to your network and smart home devices. Save your account information in a secure location.

Congratulations! You have completed initial setup of your Mozilla Things Gateway, and you’re now ready to add your smart home devices.

Keep in mind that each time you want to control the devices connected to your Things Gateway, you will need to navigate to the web address you created for it in Step 7. We recommend you save your Things Gateway as a web app on our phone and bookmark it as a web address on your laptop’s web browser for easy access.

TIP: From a phone or tablet, save your Things Gateway as a web app on the home screen for quick access.

On the web browser, type in your domain name from Step 7 [“[subdomain].mozilla-iot.org”].

  • On Android phones/tablets:
  • In Firefox: Select the “add to home” icon in the address bar (circled in red) to add an app icon to your home screen.

   

  • In Chrome: Select “Add Things to Home screen”.

  • On iPhones:  
  • In Safari: Select Share icon, and then “Add to Home Screen”.

     

III. Things: Connect Devices to your Smart Home System

A. Scan For and Add Smart Devices

You can now connect the included Smart Home Kit devices to your Things Gateway. Set up each device one at a time.

1. Go to your domain on your web browser and log in to your account. The domain should be in the format (‘subdomain.mozilla-iot.org”).

2. Pick ONE device to add and prepare it for pairing.

  • Light bulb: screw into a light fixture.
  • Smart plug: plug into an outlet.  
  • Door sensor: remove tab from battery to power on.
  • Motion sensor: remove tab from battery to power on.

 Pull out tab to power sensors on.

 

3. From the main “Things” page, select the  button at the bottom right corner. The gateway will begin scanning for nearby devices.

4. The new device will appear on the Things scan. Rename the device, then select ‘Save’ to add it and ‘Done’ when you are finished.

💡TIP: When naming your smart devices, we recommend using a name that helps you remember where they are located in your home. For example, “Bedroom Light”.

5. Repeat steps 2-4 for each device.

B. Control Your Things

Learn how you can interact with your smart home devices using the Mozilla Things Gateway.

4. Devices are displayed on the Things screen. You can toggle on and off the light bulb and smart plug from this screen, and read the current state of the door sensor and motion sensor.

5. To view and control specific details about a device, choose the icon on the top-right of each device.

6. To edit a device’s name or remove it, select the  icon in the bottom right-hand corner.

IV. Rules: Automate Your Home

Now that your Mozilla Things Gateway is set up and your smart things are connected, you can start automating your home for your convenience by creating ‘Rules’. Practice creating a rule by following the next few steps.

A. Create a Rule

Navigate to “Rules” on the Things menu.

1. Start in the top left hand corner. To the left of the paintbrush, create a name for your rule. As an example, create the rule: “At 10pm, turn off bedroom light”.

Don’t worry about the “If” statement below the Rule name, this will populate as you add your devices to the Rule space.

2. In Rule creation, the basic logic is: if given input A, then do output B (you can also change “if” to “while”).

Let’s start by grabbing our input: time. Drag the ‘clock’ from the bottom of the screen to the left side of the Rule space. Since we want something to occur at 10pm, set the time to ‘10pm’.

3. Next we select our output: the smart light bulb. Drag the ‘light’ you want to turn off at 10pm to the right side.

4. To complete the rule, choose the action you want the output (smart lightbulb) to take when our input (10pm) occurs. In this example, we want the bedroom light to turn “Off” at 10pm. Go to the light's drop down menu and select ‘Off’.

The clock and light bulb rectangles should now be connected by a black line, and the “If” statement under your Rule name should be updated to reflect this pairing.

B. Turn On/Off a Rule

5. From the Rules view, you can see all of your Rules at a glance, and toggle them on and off. For example, you might want to turn off a Rule when you are away on vacation.

You’ve just created your first Rule. Try editing it to your desired effect or create a new one, such as turning on the light when the door sensor changes to “open”.

V. Floorplan: Map out your devices

The Floorplan allows you to view your devices as they are positioned within your home. It offers an alternative way to monitor and control your smart home devices.

A. Create a Floor Plan

Sketch a floor plan of your home. You can draw one by hand or use an illustrator tool.

💡TIP: Save your digital drawing as an SVG file with white lines and a transparent background, using a tool like Inkscape or Sketch, for a minimalist look.

 

B. Upload a Floor Plan

Select the pencil icon at the bottom right to upload the image of your floor plan.

💡TIP: If you sketched your floor plan by hand, you can take a picture of it from your phone and upload it directly to the Gateway through your phone’s browser.

C. Map out your devices

While still in edit mode, map out your devices by dragging them from the top of the screen to the desired location on the floor plan.

Select the check button  when you have finished editing.

                

You can toggle your devices on and off from this floor plan view, or long press on a device to view more details about the device.

VI. Add-Ons: Extend your Gateway’s Capabilities

The gateway has an add-ons system so that you can extend its capabilities. A few add-ons are installed by default (Web Thing, Zigbee, and Z-Wave) so that your Gateway will work with the devices in the kit, but you can add support for additional Add-ons in Settings.

A. Locate Add-Ons

From the Settings menu, select Add-Ons.

B. Enable more Add-Ons

Click the button to discover more add-ons, and select ‘ + Add ‘ to enable the desired add-on. For example, if you have TP-Link or HomeKit compatible devices at home, you can install their add-ons, then configure them to connect to and be managed by your Mozilla Gateway as well.

New add-ons will continue to be developed to enable control of existing devices. Let us know if you have smart devices not yet supported.

VII. Experiments

Try out experimental new features like the Smart Assistant in Experiments.

A. Enable Smart Assistant

From the Settings menu, select Experiments, and then check Smart Assistant.

B. Use Smart Assistant (Experimental)

The smart assistant, accessible from the menu, allows you to control your home via a chat style interface, either by typing or using your voice.

You can give it commands like “Turn the kitchen light on” and it will respond to you to confirm the action. So far it can understand a basic set of commands to turn devices on and off, set levels, set color and set color temperatures.

The first time you click on the microphone icon, your browser will ask for permission to use your computer’s microphone. From the popup dialog, click the “Remember this decision” checkbox then select “Allow”.

For support, please sign up to our IoT Discourse forum (https://discourse.mozilla.org/c/iot) or email iot@mozilla.com.

Appendix

A. How to Factory Reset your Devices  

Door Sensor

  1. Remove the cover of the Sensor.
  2. Remove the CR2 battery (may need a small screwdriver to dislodge it).
  3. Hold down the small button inside the Sensor’s enclosure while re-inserting the battery.
  4. Release the button when the LED is lit.
  5. The LED will blink while attempting to connect.

Motion Sensor

  1. Remove the cover of the Sensor.
  2. Remove the CR2 battery (may need a small screwdriver to dislodge it).
  3. Hold down the small button inside the Sensor’s enclosure while re-inserting the battery.
  4. Release the button when the LED is lit.
  5. The LED will blink while attempting to connect.

Smart Plug

  1. Press and hold down the Connect button on the top of the Outlet for 5 seconds.
  2. Release the button when the LED light on the front starts blinking blue.
  3. Once the Outlet has been reset, the LED will begin to blink green and red, indicating that the Outlet is attempting to connect.

Smart Bulb

  1. Turn the bulb on then off, 6 times in a row, in rapid succession.
  2. The bulb should briefly flash once to show it has been successfully reset.        

For Samsung SmartThings (everything except the light bulb), you can also try the following wiki page for additional troubleshooting: https://github.com/mozilla-iot/wiki/wiki/Pairing-SmartThings-sensors
